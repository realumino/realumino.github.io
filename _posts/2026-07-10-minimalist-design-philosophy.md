Minimalism isn't about having less for the sake of it. It's about making room for what matters.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

## What minimalism means in design

A minimalist interface removes distractions. Every element earns its place:

- **Typography first** — let the content speak
- **Generous whitespace** — give elements room to breathe
- **Limited palette** — a few colors, used deliberately
- **Purposeful motion** — animation that guides, not distracts

## Applying it here

This blog follows those principles. The focus is on the writing — clean type, comfortable reading width, nothing flashy. The language switcher and navigation are there when you need them, invisible when you don't.

A good reading experience is one you don't notice.
